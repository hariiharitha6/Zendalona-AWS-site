Certification path

AWS certifications are organized into four tiers. Most people start at the bottom and work up.

Foundational

Cloud Practitioner

Core cloud concepts, AWS services and billing basics. No prior experience needed.

Associate

Solutions Architect & others

Role-based skills in architecture, development, operations or data.

Professional

Solutions Architect / DevOps

Advanced, production-level design and automation skills.

Specialty

Security, networking, ML

Deep expertise in one focused technical domain.

Promotional credits explained

How AWS promotional credit works

Promotional credit offsets your AWS bill for a fixed amount and a fixed time window. It is not cash, and unused credit does not roll over indefinitely — most awards are valid for around 12 months.

  • Who qualifiesNonprofits, researchers and students, depending on the specific program.
  • How it's redeemedApplied automatically in your AWS Billing dashboard once approved.
  • What it doesn't coverReserved Instances, domain purchases and some support fees.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a credit card to start AWS Educate?

No. AWS Educate is designed to be usable without a credit card, which is one of the main reasons it suits students.

Can my school apply for the Nonprofit Credit Program?

No. Schools, colleges and universities are directed to AWS Educate instead — the Nonprofit Credit Program is reserved for registered nonprofits.

Do AWS certifications expire?

Yes. Certifications are valid for three years, after which you retake the current exam or qualify through a higher-level certification.

Is this page an official AWS site?

No. This page is maintained by Zendalona as an independent guide, with links out to the official AWS pages for every program listed.
